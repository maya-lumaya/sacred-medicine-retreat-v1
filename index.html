The Three Medicines

Ancient plants, held with care.

Each medicine is offered in a guided group ceremony, with thorough preparation, support, and integration.

№ I
Nicotiana rustica

Rapéh

Amazonian snuff · group ceremony

Amazonian tobacco snuff, used for generations to clear the mind and arrive fully. We open each retreat with it.

Rapéh is a finely ground blend of Amazonian tobacco and tree ash, used by indigenous tribes in the Amazon for generations. In ceremony, in prayer, before important decisions. It is administered through the nose by a facilitator, and its effect is immediate: a sharp clearing of mental noise, a drop into the body, a sudden quiet.

Many people describe it as a reset. Within minutes, the mind stops racing. The breath deepens. There is a sense of being anchored here, in this body, in this moment.

We use it to open the retreat: to mark a threshold, set an intention, and arrive fully before the deeper work begins.

№ II
Banisteriopsis caapi · Psychotria viridis

Changa

Visionary plant blend · group ceremony

A smokable blend of DMT-containing plants and ayahuasca vine. A contained visionary experience, around 20 to 40 minutes.

Changa is a smokable blend of DMT-containing plants and ayahuasca vine. The vine contains natural MAO inhibitors, which soften the onset and extend the experience to around 20 to 40 minutes.

DMT occurs naturally in many plants and, in trace amounts, in the human body itself. It is one of the most potent psychedelic compounds known. People commonly report vivid inner landscapes, a dissolution of the boundary between self and surroundings, and a sense of encountering something that feels deeply familiar, as though returning to a place that was always there. Emotional material that has been dormant for years can surface quickly and completely.

In a ceremonial setting, with the ayahuasca vine softening the onset, the experience becomes more navigable. The visionary depth is still there, but there is more time to be with it. Many people describe a strong sense of presence and connection, to themselves, to others, to something larger.

Unlike longer ceremonies, changa works in a compressed window. This makes integration more immediate. What arose during the experience is often still close to the surface when the group comes back together to share and reflect. For many people, that proximity between experience and integration is where the real work happens.

№ III
Psilocybe cubensis

Psilocybin

Sacred mushroom · group ceremony

Sacred mushrooms with a long history of ceremonial use, and a growing body of clinical research behind them.

Psilocybin is the active compound in certain mushrooms, used in healing traditions for thousands of years. In recent years it has become one of the most researched substances in psychiatry.

Studies at Johns Hopkins, Imperial College London, and NYU have shown significant reductions in depression and anxiety, including in people for whom nothing else had worked. In multiple trials, a single guided session produced effects lasting six months or more. Researchers found that psilocybin quiets the brain's default mode network, the part responsible for rumination and self-criticism, while increasing neural flexibility and openness.

In ceremony, the experience tends to surface what needs attention: old grief, unresolved emotions, moments of unexpected clarity. Most people describe a lasting shift in how they relate to themselves and others. We work with psilocybin in a carefully prepared group setting, with close support throughout.

A renovated Savoyard chalet in the mountains, one hour from Geneva. Clean lines, open views, quiet surroundings. A space to do profound inner work.

The lodge was built in 2025 from natural materials, mostly wood, in the mountains above Geneva. It is warm and simple and quiet.
